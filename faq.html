Frequently Asked Questions

What Type of Bin Do I need?

There are three bin types - check here to see which one you need.

General Rubbish: 2m², 3m², 4.5m², 6m², 7.5m² and 9m²
Household rubbish, garden waste, building waste, wood, plastic, cardboard, office waste, furniture, home appliances etc.

Soil and Clay: 2m² and 3m²
Soil and clay only

Concrete: 2m² and 3m²
Bricks, rocks, concrete, scoria, stones, asphalt, sand, concrete roof tiles etc

What is a Cubic Metre?

A cubic metre is a standard unit of measurement.
One cubic metre is the amount that would be held in a cube that is one metre wide by one metre high by one metre deep.

Can I mix concrete, bricks and soil with my rubbish?

No, we have separate bins.
One for concrete, one for soil and clay and our general rubbish bins are for everything else, including garden waste.

How long can I have my bin for?

Bin hire is for up to 5 days but can be left longer by prior arrangement - additional charges apply.

How and When do I pay?

All bins must be paid for on or before delivery.

Credit Card - this can be done on this website.

Cash or Cheque on delivery.

Internet Banking - funds need to show in our account on the day of delivery.

Please note: Unless by prior arrangement: NO PAYMENT, NO BIN.

What if noboy is home when I want the bin delivered?

You will have to leave detailed instructions on where to place the bin and payment will need to be arranged.

How high can I fill the skip bin?

Level to the top. Think safety!!
If the bin is overloaded it will have to be levelled before we can transport it to comply with LTSA and OSH rules and regulations.

Do we have weight limits on our bins?

Yes we do on General Rubbish bins but not on Concrete or Soil Bins. For more information click here to view all our bins and their weight limits.

Is there a minimum diveway access width?

Yes, a minimum driveway width of 2.6m is required.

Can I move the bin?

NO! once placed the bin must not be moved.
If moved when empty you will not be able to move the bin back and the driver may not be able to pick up the bin again.
Any damage done to the bin or property by a bin being moved will be the responsibility of the hirer.

Can I have the bin left on the road?

Unfortunately we are unable to do this as it is a road hazard.
The only exemption is if you have obtained a permit from your local council before hand.
